# Juízes — garantias, deveres, carreira (constitucional)

Constitutional framework for the Brazilian judiciary: guarantees,
duties, entry, promotion, and administrative autonomy. This file
covers the CF/LOMAN provisions that apply across all branches of
the judiciary. For **judicial career mechanics** (entrância,
promoção, remoção, lotação, quinto constitucional), see
`carreira-juizes.md`. For **CNJ disciplinary oversight**, see
`cnj-administracao-judicial.md`. For **MP guarantees** (parallel
but distinct), see `ministerio-publico.md`.

**Topics / keywords**: juízes, garantias constitucionais,
vitaliciedade, inamovibilidade, irredutibilidade, promoção,
ingresso, concurso, quinto constitucional, subsídio, aposentadoria,
remoção, autonomia administrativa, LOMAN, vedações, deveres.

**Snapshot as of 2026**: constitutional guarantees are stable
(`CF.95`). `LOMAN` (LC 35/1979) governs the organic structure;
some provisions are outdated but remain formally in force pending
comprehensive reform.

For the parallel MP guarantees, see `ministerio-publico.md` §1.

---

## 1. Constitutional guarantees (`CF.95`)

Three guarantees protect judicial independence:

- **Vitaliciedade** — removal only by judicial decision. Acquired
  after 2 years of service for first-instance judges (`LOMAN.22`);
  immediate for STF, STJ, TST, TRT, TJ members appointed directly
  (`LOMAN.25`–`LOMAN.29`).
- **Inamovibilidade** — cannot be transferred without consent, except
  by public-interest decision of the competent órgão colegiado
  (`LOMAN.30`–`LOMAN.31`). See `carreira-juizes.md` for the practical
  mechanics of remoção.
- **Irredutibilidade de subsídio** (`LOMAN.32`).

## 2. Vedações (`CF.95.§unico`)

Judges are forbidden from:

- Holding another public or private position (except teaching).
- Receiving payments or fees from litigants (*custas ou participações*).
- Engaging in political activity.
- Receiving *auxílios* from parties or interested persons.
- Practicing advocacia in the court where they served, for 3 years
  after leaving (*quarentena*).

## 3. Entry (`CF.93.I`)

- **Concurso público de provas e títulos**: competitive examination
  for first-instance entry. Minimum 3 years of legal practice required.
- **Quinto constitucional** (`CF.94`): 1/5 of seats in TJs, TRFs, and
  TRTs reserved for lawyers (OAB) and MP members with 10+ years of
  career/practice, from *listas sêxtuplas* organized by OAB/MP.

## 4. Promoção (`CF.93.II`–`CF.93.III`)

- Alternating between **antiguidade** and **merecimento**.
- For merecimento: must be in the top 1/5 of the list and have 2 years
  in the entrância. Objective criteria required.
- See `carreira-juizes.md` for the practical entrância system.

## 5. Other constitutional rules

- **Subsídio** (`CF.93.V`): ceiling linked to STF minister salary.
- **Aposentadoria** (`CF.93.VI`): compulsory at 75 (EC 88/2015).
- **Residência** (`CF.93.VII`): judges must reside in their comarca.
- **Remoção** (`CF.93.VIII`): interest-based transfer only after 3 years.
- **Publicity** (`CF.93.IX`): all decisions public and *fundamentadas*;
  exceptions only for privacy or public interest.
- **Administrative acts** (`CF.93.X`): tribunal administrative decisions
  must be public and motivated.
- **Órgão especial** (`CF.93.XI`): tribunals with 25+ members may
  constitute an órgão especial (min 11, max 25 members).
- **Dimensionamento** (`CF.93.XIII`): number of judges proportional to
  caseload and population.
- **Distribuição imediata** (`CF.93.XV`): cases must be distributed
  immediately upon filing.

## 6. Tribunal autonomy (`CF.96`, `CF.99`)

- `CF.96.I`: tribunals have self-organizing authority — elect leadership,
  create varas, propose budget, organize secretarias.
- `CF.99`: administrative and financial autonomy; judiciary proposes its
  own budget within LDO limits.
